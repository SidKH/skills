# Fake Social Proof

- Link: https://www.deceptive.design/types/fake-social-proof

## Definition

Use fabricated or exaggerated reviews, testimonials, or recent-activity messages to make a product appear more trusted or more popular than the evidence supports.

## Audit Cues

- randomized names, locations, timestamps, or purchase events in popups
- testimonial or review data that is generated locally rather than sourced from real records
- admin controls for fake purchase notices, review inflation, or synthetic activity feeds
- copy claiming recent user actions without traceable event data
- components designed to simulate demand through invented social activity

## Generic Example

A floating popup claims that a named person in a city purchased an item minutes ago, but the implementation generates the name, place, and timestamp from random values.
