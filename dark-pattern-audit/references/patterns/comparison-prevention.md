# Comparison Prevention

- Link: [https://www.deceptive.design/types/comparison-prevention](https://www.deceptive.design/types/comparison-prevention)

## Definition

Make product comparison unnecessarily difficult by combining features and prices in inconsistent ways, hiding important details, or forcing the user to jump across multiple views to understand tradeoffs.

## Audit Cues

- pricing or plan tables with inconsistent units, bundled features, or missing totals
- details hidden behind separate drawers, modals, tabs, or accordions for each option
- taxes, fees, seat counts, or feature limits shown for some plans but omitted for others
- special low-price offers that require extra clicks while premium offers are fully summarized
- copy that pushes choice before full plan details are visible together

## Generic Example

A pricing page shows three plans. Two include taxes in the visible price, one does not. The cheaper option is hidden behind a separate modal with less information than the higher-priced plans, forcing the user to memorize details before returning.
